About Me

I am interested in learning-based robotics, especially autonomous navigation, Vision-Language-Action models, and reinforcement learning-based robot systems that enable robots to understand goals, plan actions, and interact with the physical world.

Through my major in Biosystems Engineering, I have built a foundation in mechanics, control, machine design, robotics, and electrical circuits. Through my double major in Artificial Intelligence, I am strengthening my background in linear algebra, statistics, data structures, programming, and machine learning. This background helps me study robotics from both the physical system side and the algorithmic intelligence side.

My experiences include autonomous navigation and perception system development for agricultural robots, reinforcement learning-based quadruped locomotion and manipulation research, and humanoid robotics projects. These experiences shaped my interest in robots that go beyond predefined motions and adapt to changing goals and environments.

Current interests: autonomous navigation, Vision-Language-Action models, reinforcement learning, robot manipulation, and embodied AI.
